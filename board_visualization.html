Why Flat Tuple?

Immutable — tuples can't be mutated, safe to copy into MCTS tree nodes
O(1) access — single index lookup vs nested list[row][col]
Fast copy — tuple slicing creates new board states efficiently during rollouts
Column heights — separate height tuple gives O(1) piece placement

MCTS — The Four Phases (1000× per move)

      


        

          
1️⃣

          
Selection

          

            Start at root. Walk down the tree picking the child with the highest UCT score:
          

          

            w/n + C√(ln(N)/n)
          

          

            w/n = exploit (high win rate)

            C√(ln(N)/n) = explore (less visited)
          

        


        

          
2️⃣

          
Expansion

          

            When we reach a node with untried moves, pick one randomly and add it as a new child node in the tree.
          

          

            The child stores a copy of the board state after that move.
          

        


        

          
3️⃣

          
Simulation

          

            From the new node, play random moves for both sides until someone wins or it's a draw. This is the "rollout."
          

          

            Random play averages out — good positions tend to win more rollouts.
          

        


        

          
4️⃣

          
Backpropagation

          

            Walk back up to root. Every node on the path gets its visit count incremented and win count updated.
          

          

            After 1000 iterations, pick the root's most-visited child.
